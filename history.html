History
CRICKET CAREER

Introduction to Cricket


Virat developed an early interest in cricket, and his family recognized his talent and passion for the sport. His father supported his cricketing ambitions and enrolled him in the West Delhi Cricket Academy when he was just 9 years old. Rajkumar Sharma, his first coach, played a significant role in shaping Kohli's cricketing skills.

School Cricket


Kohli attended Vishal Bharti Public School in Delhi, where he balanced his academics with cricket. His exceptional performances in school cricket caught the attention of scouts and coaches, paving the way for his entry into Delhi's age-group cricket.

Domestic Cricket and Youth Tournaments


Kohli progressed through the ranks in Delhi's age-group cricket and eventually made his way to the Ranji Trophy, representing Delhi in first-class cricket. He also played in various youth tournaments, showcasing his talent on the national stage.

Under-19 Cricket


In 2006, Virat Kohli captained the Indian Under-19 cricket team in the ICC Under-19 Cricket World Cup held in Sri Lanka. India emerged victorious in the tournament, and Kohli's leadership and batting performances earned him recognition.

National Team Debut


Virat Kohli made his debut for the Indian national team in August 2008 during an ODI against Sri Lanka. He quickly established himself as a key player in the Indian batting lineup, displaying a combination of skill, temperament, and aggression.

Virat Kohli's journey from a young and talented cricketer to one of the best batsmen in the world has been remarkable, marked by numerous records and achievements in international cricket.
